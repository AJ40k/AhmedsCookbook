Level Exprienced
Preparation time 40 mins
Cooking Time 4 hours
Yield 5 servings

Ingredients:

  • 1.4 kg of chicken legs
  • 2 and a half teaspoons of salt and half a teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 cups of red wine
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 sprigs of fresh thmye, with the leaves chopped only
  • 110 g of bacon dices into quater inch pieces
  • 3 tablespoons virgin olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 large carrot, diced
  • 220 g of white mushrooms, halved
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoons of tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons of brandy
  • 3 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 250 g of peeled pearl onions
  • 2 slices of white bread
  • Quater cup of chopped parsley, for garnish

Preparation:

Step 1: Season the chicken in a bowl with the salt and pepper. Add the wine, bay leaf and thyme. Cover and refrigerate for 1 hour.

Step 2: In a large dutch oven, heat the lardons over medium heat until all the fat has rendered out. Remove the cripsy lardons to dry over paper towels.

Step 3: Remove the chicken form the marinade and dry it, reserve the marinade. Fry the chicken in batches and transfer to a plate as it browns.

Step 4: Add the onion, mushrooms and a quater teaspoon of salt to the pot. Cook until the vegetables are lightly browned, preventing any burning, for 10 minutes

Step 5:Stir in garlic and tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes, add in the flour and cook for another 2 minutes. Kill the heat, add the brandy and cook for 1 minute. Add reserved marinade and reduce it down to about half.

Step 6: Add the chicken, any juices and half the lardons to the pot. Cover and simmer over low heat for 1 hour. Uncover the pot and simmer for another 15 minutes and the salt and pepper for if necessary.

Step 7: Melt 1 tablespoon of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil in a large non-stick skillet. Add the pearl onions, salt and sugar to taste. Fry the onions unti tender and move to one sidde. Add the mushrooms and continue to cook until brown. After 8 minutes, remove the onions and mushrooms and wipe the skillet.

Step 8: Add 2 tablespoons of butter to the skillet. Add the bread and fry until golden. 2 minutes each side to avoid burning. Removes from skillet and sprinkle with salt.

Step 9: For serving, dip croutons in wine sauce and cover with parsley. Add pearl onions, mushrooms and other half of the cooked lardons to the pot. Baste the pot with wine to avoid any burning and scrape the fond at the bottom of the pot. Sprinkle with parsley and serve with the croutons on top.
