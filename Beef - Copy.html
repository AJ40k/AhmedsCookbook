Preparation time 1 hr
Cooking Time 7 hrs
Yield 7 servings

Ingredients:

For the mushrooms:

  • One and a half pound of white mushrooms
  • 2 shallots, peeled and chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, peeled and chopped
  • leaves of 2 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • half a pound of unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons of olive-oil
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

For the beef:

  • 3 to 4 pound beef tenderloin(filet mignon)
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 10 slices of prosciutto
  • leaves of 6 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 2 tablespoons dijon mustard
  • Flour for puff pastry
  • one pound of puff pastry
  • 2 lightly beaten eggs
  • chives, for garnish
  • Green peppercorn sauce, as given below

Green peppercorn sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 sliced shallots
  • 2 cloves of garlic, peeled and smashed
  • 3 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • one cup of brandy
  • one box of beef stock
  • 2 cups of cream
  • 2 tablespoons of grain mustard
  • half a cup of green peppercorns, in brine.

preparation

For the mushrooms:

Add the mushrroms, shallots, garlic and thyme to a food processor and pulse until finely chopped. Add olive oil and butter to a pan over medium heat. Add the mushroom mixture and saute for 10 minutes until the water has evaporated. Add salt and pepper to taste, then set aside to cool.

For the beef:

Tie the beef in strings so that it holds a cylindrical shape. Cover the in oil, season with salt and pepper and sear the tenderloin in a hot skillet with oil olive. While the beef is searing, arrange the prosciutto in a rectangle on top of a sheet of plastic wrap. Cover the prosciutto in the mixture of mushrooms. Place the seared beef atop the mixture the remove the strings. Smear the beef with dijon mustard and carefully use the plastic wrap to encompass the tenderloin with the porciutto. Tuck in the beef using the prociutto and make sure its completely covered. Use the wrap to close off the ends of the beef and se t it in the fridge to cool and maintain its shape.

For the greenpeppercorn sauce:

Add olive oil to the pan after removing the beef. Add shallots, thyme and garlic, saute for 3 minutes. Add beef stock and reduce about half. Strain out the solids and add 2 cups of cream and mustard. Reduce about half again and turn off the heat, add the green peppercorns.

For the baking:

Preheat oven to 220 C. On a slightly floured surface, roll out the puff pastry to about quater-inch thickness. Remove the beef from the refrigerator and set it into the center of the pastry and fold the pastry over the beef. Brus the top with egg wash and seal. Cut seams into the top to allow steam to vent. Place this on the baking sheet. Bake for 45 to 50 minutes until the pastry is golden brown and the beef registers 52 C. Remove from the oven a nd set aside to rest for 30 minutes. Cut into thick slices and serve with the green peppercorn sauce.

History about Beef Wellington:

  • It originally was a pie of english origin, made out of a filet and covered with puff pastry. Some recipes mention wrapping the beef in a crepe.
  • By the time the name beef wellington was popularised, meat baked in pastry was already a well-established in English cuisine. The beef welligtoon of present is only a rebranding of a classical continental dish.
  • The "Welligton" part of the dish name relates to wrapping the beef in pastry. Hence, alot of dishes include the welligton name as they are wrapped in pastry.
